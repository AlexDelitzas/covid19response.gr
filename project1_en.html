Project Shield

PROTECTIVE FACE SHIELDS FOR HEALTH CARE WORKERS LAST UPDATE: 02/04/2020 διαβαστε στα ελληνικα

AIM OF THE PROJECT


  • Design solutions to be used as a substitute for protective face shields and be mass-produced promptly in large quantities per week.
  • Open source technical drawings, accessible to everyone for free licensed under a Creative Commons (4.0 International License) Attribution-NonCommercial.
  • Possibility of commercial use by potential manufacturers on condition that the pricing is reasonable.
  • Technical support of concurrent design for potential manufacturers in order for the manufacture to start in different regions of Greece.
  • Coordination with medical associations and hospitals in order to meet the needs of front line health professionals as soon as possible.

CURRENT NEEDS


  • We request everyone who has access to production assets that can be used for the manufacturing of “Face shield consisting of two units of transparent PET sheets” as described below to come in contact with us.
  • Specifically, we are looking for manufacturers who are able to process PET sheets of 0.4mm with press cutter (optimally) or with closed type CO2 laser cutting machine.

1. CREATING PROTOTYPES


Inspired by the technical drawing of the face shield that was published on Hackaday community by the Polish Konrad Klepacki and Mateusz Dyrda [link], we designed a face shield substitute which could be mass-produced fast in Greece.

The technical drawing is an extremely simple manufacture of a protective face shield consisting of a transparent PET sheet of 0.4mm width. The shield consists of two parts made of the same material; a transparent front and a strip that turns into a rim for the head [DXF files and manufacturing instructions link].

During the assembly, the front can be attached to the rim with a double sided tape or a stapler. For more comfort, a polyethylene sheet can be added to the interior part of the rim that touches the forehead [instructions of assembly link].

For the testing of the technical drawing we manufactured prototypes by cutting PET sheet of 0.4mm width with a closed type CO2 laser cutter.



2. ASSESSMENT BY DOCTORS


This particular technical drawing of face shield was assessed first by the Panhellenic Medical Association and by doctors of AHEPA University Hospital, thus the length of the front was slightly modified. The modified technical drawing was evaluated by doctors of Papanikolaou General Hospital of Thessaloniki, Ippokrateio General Hospital of Thessaloniki and General Hospital of Kastoria.

We tested the durability of the material and the potential of reusability of the face shield at Saint Luke’s Clinic in Thessaloniki, using four different kinds of disinfectants (two gels and two washables) that are shown below. PET sheet did not blur nor change color during the test. The same happened with polyethylene sheet.



3. PRODUCTION METHODS


The technical drawing can be manufactured in any production site that has access to equipment capable of cutting PET sheet of 0.4mm.

We tested the cut with a closed type CO2 laser cutter at the premises of Rodia Event Decoration in Edessa. We also attempted cutting the PET sheet with press paper cutter at the premises of Soulis ΑΒΕΕ in Skydra.

The process of cutting with the press allows the cut of many PET sheets at the same time. This increases significantly the capacity of production instead of the cut from a laser cutter, which requires one sheet at a time to be cut.

4. CONCLUSIONS


  • We designed a technical drawing of an extremely simple face shield, consisting of two units of transparent PET sheet (plastic - polyethylene terephthalate).
  • Today, PET is a material in stock in Greece and can be easily processed by small and medium industries that exist throughout the country.
  • The design allows the face shields to be reused on condition that the double sided tapes that connect the two parts (rim and front) are replaced after the disinfection.
  • The manufacture of the face shield has low production cost which is estimated to discourage profiteering attempts.

SOLUTION 1: FACE SHIELD BASED ON THE TECHNICAL DRAWING “PRUSA PROTECTIVE FACE SHIELD - RC2”

  1. 1. PROTOTYPES
  2. 2. ASSESSMENT
  3. 3. PRODUCTION

  5. 5. MEMBERS

1. CREATING PROTOTYPES


We based our project on the technical drawing of the protective face shield that the Czech firm Prusa Research developed [link to Prusa technical drawing].

This technical drawing is available free of charge under the license terms of Creative Commons (4.0 International License) Attribution-NonCommercial, which allows modification of the technical drawing (Remix), but prohibits the commercial use without the permission of Prusa Research.

We printed Prusa’s technical drawing on a 3D printer (Fused Deposition Modelling) using PLA filament as the printing material and following the directions that Prusa provided. Having our 3D printer functioning 24 hours, 6 units were manufactured.

Furthermore, we cut panels of transparent PET (polyethylene terephthalate) plastic sheet of 0.4mm width, for the front of the shield using a laser cutter.

We realised that the front panel could not attach to the mending slots of the rim that we had printed. There had to be a modification of the original technical drawing by increasing the diameter of the hole of the PET sheet [modified technical drawing link].



2. ASSESSMENT BY DOCTORS


We came in contact with the president of the Panhellenic Medical Association, Mr. Athanasios Eksadaktylos, and on 23/3/2020 we distributed some of the prototypes that we manufactured to the administrator of AHEPA University Hospital, Mr. Panagiotis Panteliadis, in order to be evaluated. The medical staff positively assessed the design as for its functionality with no negative comment.



3. PRODUCTION METHODS


This specific technical drawing of a face shield has already found great acceptance among communities interested in 3D printing around the world. Prusa Research has started the manufacturing in the printer farm of its facilities, in order to meet the needs of the hospitals in Czech Republic for ocular protection.

Given that a small 3D printer cannot manufacture over 10 units per day, in order to achieve the manufacturing of 1000 units per day from one single manufacturer there must be a printer farm of 100 printers.

An alternative solution would be a process of distributed 3D printing from individual volunteers with the gathering of the printed parts and their assembly into one central point. It should be noted that this process increases the danger of infection of the printed parts of the manufacture from the individual volunteers, since it is hard (and certainly harder than central manufacturing) to preclude some of the volunteers from carrying the COVID-19 virus.

A different manufacturing method would be with the use of injection molding after the manufacturing of the mold. This can be done with the typical process of the steel mold which has high manufacturing cost and requires much time for its completion, in order for the manufacturing to start. A positive aspect of this method is that the manufacturer can achieve the producing of thousands of units per day.

The method that we are examining today is the producing with injection molding using mold that has been manufactured with SLA 3D printing (resin polymerisation) [link]. This method would allow fast manufacturing of the mold and fast production launch with large numbers of units per day. A negative aspect is the high rate with which the mold should be replaced, due to its low durability. However, the cost for the production of approximately ten thousand face shields is estimated lower than the cost of injection molding with steel mold.

4. CONCLUSIONS


  • Prusa’s technical drawing has been accepted for use from the doctors.
  • Its production through 3D printing is prohibitively slow.
  • For the production of large quantities of face shields per day, as the current situation demands with the deficiencies in the greek hospitals, the solution of the production with injection molding is considered necessary.
  • A method which could combine fast production launch with a great capacity is injection molding with a resin mold instead of steel.
